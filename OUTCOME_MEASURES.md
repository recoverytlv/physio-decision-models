# Outcome Measures — Validated Tools Used at Recovery TLV

## Purpose

Objective outcome measurement is a non-negotiable component of the clinical decision system. Subjective improvement alone cannot justify treatment continuation. This document lists the validated outcome measures used to track progress and inform decisions.

## General Principles

1. **Baseline before treatment** — Every measure is taken at initial assessment before any intervention
2. **Repeated at defined intervals** — Re-measured at trial window end (session 3-5) and at each continuation decision point
3. **MCID matters** — Change must exceed the Minimal Clinically Important Difference to count as meaningful
4. **Condition-specific + generic** — Use at least one condition-specific and one generic measure per case

## Physical Measures

### Range of Motion (ROM)
- **Tool:** Goniometer, inclinometer
- **Application:** All musculoskeletal conditions with ROM limitations
- **MCID:** Generally ≥10% improvement from baseline
- **Decision link:** ROM improvement is a primary CONTINUE criterion

### Strength Testing
- **Tool:** Manual muscle testing (MMT), handheld dynamometer, 1RM estimation
- **Application:** Post-surgical, weakness-related dysfunction, tendinopathy
- **MCID:** ≥1 MMT grade or ≥20% dynamometer improvement
- **Decision link:** Strength gains support CONTINUE, stasis suggests hypothesis revision

## Functional Performance Tests

| Test | What It Measures | Population | MCID |
|------|-----------------|------------|------|
| Timed Up and Go (TUG) | Mobility, balance, fall risk | Geriatric, post-surgical | 2.5 seconds |
| 30-Second Sit-to-Stand | Lower limb strength/endurance | Knee OA, hip OA, geriatric | 2-3 repetitions |
| 6-Minute Walk Test (6MWT) | Aerobic capacity, endurance | Deconditioning, stenosis, cardiac rehab | 50 meters |
| Single Leg Stance | Balance, proprioception | Ankle instability, fall risk | 5 seconds |
| Y-Balance Test | Dynamic balance, asymmetry | Lower limb, return-to-sport | ≥4 cm asymmetry reduction |
| Stair Climb Test | Functional lower limb capacity | Knee/hip conditions | Time or step count improvement |
| Grip Strength | Hand/forearm function | Hand conditions, general health marker | 6 kg |
| Hop Tests (single, triple, crossover) | Power, confidence, symmetry | ACL rehab, return-to-sport | ≥90% limb symmetry index |

## Patient-Reported Outcome Measures (PROMs)

### Region-Specific PROMs

| Measure | Region | Items | MCID | Scoring |
|---------|--------|-------|------|---------|
| Neck Disability Index (NDI) | Cervical | 10 | 5 points | 0-50 (higher = worse) |
| Oswestry Disability Index (ODI) | Lumbar | 10 | 6 points | 0-100% (higher = worse) |
| DASH (Disabilities of Arm, Shoulder, Hand) | Upper limb | 30 | 10 points | 0-100 (higher = worse) |
| SPADI (Shoulder Pain & Disability Index) | Shoulder | 13 | 8 points | 0-100 (higher = worse) |
| KOOS (Knee Injury & OA Outcome Score) | Knee | 42 | 8-10 points | 0-100 (higher = better) |
| HOOS (Hip Disability & OA Outcome Score) | Hip | 40 | 8-10 points | 0-100 (higher = better) |
| FAAM (Foot & Ankle Ability Measure) | Foot/ankle | 29 | 8 points | 0-100% (higher = better) |
| LEFS (Lower Extremity Functional Scale) | Lower limb | 20 | 9 points | 0-80 (higher = better) |
| HDI (Headache Disability Index) | Headache | 25 | 16 points | 0-100 (higher = worse) |

### Generic PROMs

| Measure | Application | MCID |
|---------|-------------|------|
| Visual Analog Scale (VAS) | Pain intensity | 1.5-2.0 cm |
| Numeric Pain Rating Scale (NPRS) | Pain intensity | 2 points |
| Patient-Specific Functional Scale (PSFS) | Any — patient-selected activities | 2 points |
| Global Rating of Change (GRoC) | Overall perceived change | ≥+3 (much better) |
| Tampa Scale of Kinesiophobia (TSK) | Fear of movement | 4 points |
| Pain Catastrophizing Scale (PCS) | Catastrophizing | 6 points |
| EQ-5D | Health-related quality of life | 0.08 (utility score) |

| Tool | Purpose | When to Use | Action |

## How Outcome Measures Inform Decisions

```
Baseline Assessment
        ↓
    TRIAL (3-5 sessions)
        ↓
    Re-measure outcomes
        ↓
    Change ≥ MCID? ──── Yes → CONTINUE
        │
        No
        ↓
    Hypothesis revision
        ↓
    Revised intervention (2-3 sessions)
        ↓
    Re-measure again
        ↓
    Change ≥ MCID? ──── Yes → CONTINUE
        │
        No
        ↓
    DISCHARGE or REFER
```

## Important Notes

1. **PROMs supplement, not replace, objective measures** — See [Subjective Report Insufficiency](models/subjective-report-insufficiency.md)
2. **Use the right tool for the right condition** — Don't use KOOS for a neck problem
3. **Track the same measures consistently** — Don't switch tools mid-treatment
4. **Document MCID status explicitly** — "NDI improved 8 points (MCID = 5) → exceeds threshold → CONTINUE"
5. **Missing data is a red flag** — If a measure cannot be taken, document why

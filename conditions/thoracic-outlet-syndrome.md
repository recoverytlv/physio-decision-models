# Thoracic Outlet Syndrome (TOS)

## Definition
Compression of neurovascular structures (brachial plexus, subclavian artery/vein) in the thoracic outlet region. Three types: neurogenic (95%), venous (3%), arterial (2%). Physiotherapy primarily manages neurogenic TOS.

## Scope Boundaries
- **IN scope:** Neurogenic TOS with musculoskeletal contributors (scalene tightness, poor posture, first rib dysfunction)
- **OUT of scope:** Vascular TOS (venous/arterial) → REFER to vascular surgery

## Red Flags → REFER or DECLINE
- Arm swelling, cyanosis, or color changes (venous TOS)
- Absent or diminished radial pulse with positional changes (arterial TOS)
- Hand ischemia or pallor
- Progressive hand muscle wasting (severe neurogenic compression)
- Subclavian steal symptoms (dizziness, syncope with arm elevation)

## Differential Diagnosis

| Feature | Neurogenic TOS | Cervical Radiculopathy | Carpal Tunnel |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Distribution | C8-T1 (ulnar side) most common | Dermatomal | Median nerve (thumb-index-middle) |
| Provocative | Roos test, EAST, arm elevation | Spurling's, ULTT | Phalen's, Tinel's |
| Posture effect | Worse with arms overhead | Variable | Not typically postural |
| Vascular signs | Possible (pulse changes) | No | No |
| Night symptoms | Variable | Common | Very common |

## Clinical Hypothesis Framework

### Scalene-Mediated Compression
- **Hypothesis:** Anterior/middle scalene tightness is compressing the brachial plexus
- **Test:** Scalene stretching, soft tissue mobilization, postural correction
- **Expected response:** Reduced arm symptoms with overhead activities within 3-5 sessions
- **If no response:** Consider first rib dysfunction or structural anomaly (cervical rib)

### Postural (Droopy Shoulder)
- **Hypothesis:** Poor scapular posture narrows the costoclavicular space
- **Test:** Scapular strengthening, postural retraining, workplace ergonomics
- **Expected response:** Symptom reduction with improved posture within 3-5 sessions
- **If no response:** REFER for imaging (cervical rib, elongated C7 transverse process)

### First Rib Dysfunction
- **Hypothesis:** Elevated or hypomobile first rib reduces the thoracic outlet space
- **Test:** First rib mobilization, scalene release
- **Expected response:** Improved arm elevation tolerance, reduced numbness
- **If no response:** REFER for specialist assessment

## Trial Protocol (TRIAL)
| Session | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | Differentiate neurogenic vs vascular, assess scalene/posture/first rib contributions |
| 2 | Targeted intervention based on hypothesis (scalene, posture, first rib) |
| 3 | Re-evaluate: Roos test duration, symptom reproduction threshold |
| 4-5 | Progress or exit |

## Decision Criteria

### CONTINUE if:
- Roos test duration improves (can hold longer before symptoms)
- Reduced frequency/intensity of arm symptoms
- Functional improvement with overhead tasks

### DISCHARGE if:
- Symptom-free with work and daily activities
- Independent maintenance program established

### REFER if:
- Vascular signs present at any point
- Progressive weakness or wasting
- No improvement after 5 sessions of targeted intervention
- Suspected structural anomaly (cervical rib)

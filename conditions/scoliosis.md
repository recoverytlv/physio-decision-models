# Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis — Physiotherapy Scope

## Definition
Lateral curvature of the spine ≥10° (Cobb angle) in adolescents with no identifiable cause. Physiotherapy role: exercise-based management (Schroth method, SEAS), postural education, monitoring, and post-surgical rehabilitation.

## Scope Boundaries
- **IN scope:** Mild-moderate curves (10-40° Cobb) with exercise-based management, brace-wearing support, post-surgical spinal rehab, adult degenerative scoliosis with pain
- **OUT of scope:** Bracing prescription (orthotist), surgical decision-making (orthopedic surgeon), curves >45-50° without surgical opinion

## Red Flags → REFER
- Rapid curve progression (>5° in 6 months in growing adolescent)
- Neurological signs (asymmetric reflexes, weakness, bowel/bladder changes)
- Pain as primary symptom in adolescent scoliosis (atypical — investigate further)
- Left thoracic curve (higher association with intraspinal pathology)
- Onset before age 10 (juvenile/infantile — different natural history)
- Skeletal immaturity with curve >25° (bracing evaluation needed)

## Clinical Hypothesis Framework

### Mild Curves (10-25° Cobb) — Skeletal Immature
- **Hypothesis:** Specific exercise can reduce curve progression risk during growth
- **Test:** Schroth-based or SEAS exercise program, 3-5x/week home program
- **Expected response:** Curve stabilization or improvement (verified by radiograph at 6-12 months)
- **If progression:** REFER for bracing evaluation

### Moderate Curves (25-40° Cobb) — With Brace
- **Hypothesis:** Exercise combined with bracing optimizes outcomes
- **Test:** Specific corrective exercises in and out of brace, breathing exercises, postural awareness
- **Expected response:** Maintained/improved curve, improved body awareness, reduced pain if present
- **If progression despite bracing + exercise:** REFER for surgical consultation

### Adult Degenerative Scoliosis with Pain
- **Hypothesis:** Pain is driven by muscle imbalance, deconditioning, or stenosis
- **Test:** Core strengthening, flexibility program, functional training
- **Expected response:** Pain reduction, improved function within 6-8 sessions
- **If no response:** See [Lumbar Spinal Stenosis](./lumbar-spinal-stenosis.md) protocol

## Exercise Approach (Schroth-Based Principles)
1. **Auto-elongation:** Active spinal lengthening
2. **3D Correction:** Addressing curve in all three planes (frontal, sagittal, transverse)
3. **Rotational Angular Breathing:** Expanding concave side of curve
4. **Muscle activation:** Strengthening convex-side muscles, releasing concave-side tightness
5. **Postural integration:** Corrected posture in daily activities

## Trial Protocol (TRIAL)
| Session | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | Assessment, Cobb angle review, posture analysis, baseline functional measures |
| 2-3 | Teach specific corrective exercises, establish home program |
| 4-5 | Re-evaluate: exercise competence, posture in ADLs, pain if relevant |
| 6+ | Ongoing monitoring (adolescent: every 3-6 months during growth) |

## Decision Criteria

### CONTINUE if:
- Patient performing home exercises correctly
- No curve progression
- Improved posture awareness
- Pain reduction (if pain was present)

### DISCHARGE if:
- Skeletal maturity reached with stable curve
- Independent exercise program
- Adult: functional goals met

### REFER if:
- Red flags present
- Curve progression >5° despite exercise
- Curve >25° in growing adolescent without brace evaluation
- Curve >40-45° — surgical consultation
- Neurological symptoms

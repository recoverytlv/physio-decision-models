# Rib Dysfunction & Costochondritis

## Definition
Musculoskeletal chest wall pain originating from costovertebral joints, costochondral junctions, or intercostal structures. Costochondritis: inflammation of costochondral junctions. Rib dysfunction: hypomobility or hypermobility of costovertebral/costotransverse joints.

## Critical Safety Note
Chest pain MUST be screened for cardiac, pulmonary, and gastrointestinal causes before musculoskeletal diagnosis. When in doubt → REFER.

## Red Flags → REFER or DECLINE (IMMEDIATE)
- **Cardiac:** Chest pain with exertion, radiation to arm/jaw, shortness of breath, diaphoresis, history of cardiac disease → ER
- **Pulmonary:** Pleuritic pain with fever, cough, dyspnea → medical evaluation
- **Pulmonary embolism:** Sudden onset chest pain + dyspnea, recent surgery/immobilization, calf pain → ER
- **Aortic dissection:** Tearing chest pain radiating to back → ER
- **GI:** Pain worse with eating, reflux symptoms → gastroenterology
- **Fracture:** Trauma history with point tenderness, crepitus → imaging
- **Malignancy:** Unexplained weight loss, night pain, history of cancer → medical evaluation

## Differentiation Table

| Feature | Musculoskeletal | Cardiac | Pulmonary | GI |
|---------|----------------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Reproducible with palpation | Yes | No | Rarely | No |
| Movement-related | Yes | With exertion | With breathing | No |
| Position-dependent | Often | Rarely | Sometimes | Sometimes |
| Response to manual Tx | Yes | No | No | No |
| Onset | Often gradual or after strain | Sudden or exertional | Variable | After eating/reflux |

## Clinical Hypothesis Framework

### Costovertebral Joint Dysfunction
- **Hypothesis:** Hypomobile costovertebral joint causes localized thoracic/chest wall pain
- **Test:** Costovertebral joint mobilization (PA glides, rib springing)
- **Expected response:** Immediate pain reduction ≥30%, improved rib cage expansion within 1-2 sessions
- **If no response:** Reassess diagnosis — consider visceral referral

### Costochondritis
- **Hypothesis:** Costochondral junction inflammation drives anterior chest pain
- **Test:** Activity modification, gentle stretching, anti-inflammatory advice (ice, positioning)
- **Expected response:** Gradual pain reduction over 2-4 weeks
- **If no response:** Consider Tietze syndrome (visible swelling) or rheumatological cause

### Intercostal Strain
- **Hypothesis:** Muscle or fascial strain from coughing, twisting, or overuse
- **Test:** Breathing retraining, gentle mobilization, progressive loading
- **Expected response:** Pain reduction with breathing and movement within 3-5 sessions

## Trial Protocol (TRIAL)
| Session | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | RULE OUT non-musculoskeletal causes. Thorough screening. Palpation reproduction. Baseline |
| 2 | Targeted mobilization or soft tissue based on hypothesis |
| 3 | Re-evaluate: pain reproduction, movement quality, breathing |
| 4-5 | Progress or exit |

## Decision Criteria

### CONTINUE if:
- Pain reduction with palpation reproduction test
- Improved rib cage mobility / breathing quality
- Functional improvement

### DISCHARGE if:
- Pain resolved
- Normal rib cage mechanics
- Independent self-management

### REFER if:
- ANY cardiac, pulmonary, or GI red flags
- No response to 3-5 sessions (consider alternative diagnosis)
- Recurrent costochondritis — rheumatological evaluation

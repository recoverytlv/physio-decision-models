# Tension-Type Headache & Cervicogenic Headache

## Definition
Headaches with musculoskeletal contribution. Tension-type headache (TTH): bilateral, pressing/tightening, mild-moderate intensity. Cervicogenic headache (CGH): unilateral, originates from cervical spine dysfunction, referred to head.

## Scope Boundaries
- **IN scope:** TTH with cervical/muscular contributors, CGH with identifiable cervical dysfunction, post-traumatic headache (musculoskeletal component)
- **OUT of scope:** Migraine management (beyond cervical contribution), cluster headache, medication overuse headache, secondary headaches from intracranial pathology

## Red Flags → REFER or DECLINE (SNOOP Mnemonic)
- **S**ystemic symptoms (fever, weight loss, cancer history)
- **N**eurological signs (papilledema, focal deficit, altered consciousness)
- **O**nset sudden ("thunderclap" — worst headache of life)
- **O**lder age (new headache >50 years — giant cell arteritis)
- **P**ositional pattern change, progressive pattern, precipitated by Valsalva

### Additional Red Flags
- Headache after head trauma with cognitive changes → post-concussion protocol
- New daily persistent headache
- Headache with jaw claudication (giant cell arteritis → REFER urgently)
- Seizure with headache

## Differentiation Table

| Feature | Tension-Type | Cervicogenic | Migraine |
|---------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Location | Bilateral, band-like | Unilateral, starts in neck | Unilateral (can be bilateral) |
| Quality | Pressing/tightening | Dull, radiating from neck | Pulsating/throbbing |
| Intensity | Mild-moderate | Moderate | Moderate-severe |
| Duration | 30 min – 7 days | Variable, hours-days | 4-72 hours |
| Aura | No | No | May have |
| Nausea/vomiting | Rare | Occasional | Common |
| Photo/phonophobia | Mild (one, not both) | Rare | Common (both) |
| Cervical involvement | Muscle tension | Joint dysfunction C0-C3 | Possible trigger |
| Response to cervical Tx | Often helps | Primary treatment | May help if cervical trigger |

## Clinical Hypothesis Framework

### Cervicogenic Headache
- **Hypothesis:** Upper cervical joint dysfunction (C0-C3) is producing referred headache
- **Test:** Upper cervical mobilization, deep neck flexor retraining, C1-2 rotation assessment
- **Expected response:** Headache frequency reduction ≥50% within 4-6 treatments
- **If no response:** Consider other headache type, medication overuse, or psychosocial factors

### Tension-Type with Muscular Component
- **Hypothesis:** Cervical/pericranial muscle tension drives headache
- **Test:** Suboccipital release, upper trapezius/SCM soft tissue work, postural correction, stress management
- **Expected response:** Reduced intensity and frequency within 3-5 sessions
- **If no response:** Consider central sensitization, medication overuse → REFER

### Post-Traumatic Headache (Musculoskeletal Component)
- **Hypothesis:** Cervical dysfunction from trauma contributes to ongoing headache
- **Test:** Cervical assessment and treatment per CGH protocol
- **Expected response:** See [Post-Concussion Protocol](./post-concussion.md)
- **If no response:** Multidisciplinary referral

## Outcome Measures
- Headache frequency (days per month)
- Headache Disability Index (HDI)
- Neck Disability Index (NDI) — for cervicogenic
- Visual Analog Scale (VAS) for intensity

## Trial Protocol (TRIAL)
| Session | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | Classification (TTH vs CGH vs mixed), cervical assessment, baseline headache diary |
| 2-3 | Targeted cervical treatment, ergonomic advice, self-management strategies |
| 4-5 | Re-evaluate headache frequency and intensity vs baseline |

## Decision Criteria

### CONTINUE if:
- Headache frequency reduced ≥30%
- Headache intensity reduced ≥2/10 VAS
- Improved cervical function (ROM, NDI)

### DISCHARGE if:
- Headache well-controlled with self-management
- Independent home program (stretching, posture, stress management)

### REFER if:
- Any SNOOP red flag
- No response to 5 sessions of targeted cervical treatment
- Suspected migraine requiring medication management
- Medication overuse headache pattern

# Sciatica and Lumbar Radiculopathy

## Condition
Sciatica, lumbar radiculopathy, and nerve root involvement with pain radiating below the knee in a dermatomal distribution.

## Scope Check
Sciatica is within physiotherapy scope when the presentation is consistent with mechanical nerve root compression or neural sensitivity. Progressive neurological deficit or cauda equina signs trigger immediate REFER.

## Red Flags (→ REFER)
- Progressive motor weakness (foot drop, inability to heel walk or toe walk)
- Bilateral radiculopathy
- Bladder or bowel dysfunction
- Saddle anaesthesia
- Rapidly worsening neurological signs

## Clinical Hypothesis Examples
- **Disc herniation with directional preference:** Nerve root compression responding to directional loading. Tested with repeated movements. Expected response: centralization of symptoms within 3 sessions.
- **Neural mechanosensitivity:** Heightened nerve sensitivity without progressive compression. Tested with graded neural mobilization. Expected response: improved straight leg raise or slump test range.
- **Foraminal stenosis:** Positional nerve root compression. Tested with flexion-based approaches and decompression. Expected response: reduced radicular symptoms in loaded positions.

## Trial Window
3–5 sessions. Objective criteria:
- Centralization or reduction of radicular symptoms
- ≥10% improvement in straight leg raise or slump test range
- Functional improvement (walking distance, sitting tolerance, stair negotiation)
- Reduction in compensatory gait patterns

## Non-Response
If radicular symptoms do not centralize or neurological signs progress, the case is referred for specialist assessment (imaging, surgical consultation). Non-response with stable neurology may warrant hypothesis revision (e.g., from disc to stenosis, or from mechanical to sensitization model).

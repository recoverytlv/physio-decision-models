# Fibromyalgia — Physiotherapy Scope

## Definition
Widespread musculoskeletal pain with central sensitization, fatigue, sleep disturbance, and cognitive symptoms. Fibromyalgia is a clinical diagnosis — there is no definitive biomarker or imaging finding.

## Scope Boundaries
- **IN scope:** Exercise prescription, graded activity, pain neuroscience education, functional restoration
- **OUT of scope:** Pharmacological management, psychological therapy (REFER if primary), rheumatological diagnosis confirmation

## Important Context
Fibromyalgia patients often have negative experiences with healthcare. Many have been told "nothing is wrong" or their pain is "all in their head." Physiotherapy must validate the reality of their pain while providing evidence-based management.

## Red Flags → REFER
- New focal neurological signs (rule out neurological condition)
- Significant weight loss, night sweats (rule out malignancy/infection)
- Symptoms better explained by inflammatory arthritis, hypothyroidism, or other systemic disease → rheumatology
- Severe depression, suicidal ideation → psychology/psychiatry urgently
- Sleep apnea suspected → sleep medicine

## Clinical Hypothesis Framework

### Deconditioning Hypothesis
- **Hypothesis:** Physical deconditioning perpetuates pain-fatigue cycle
- **Test:** Graded exercise program starting well below symptom threshold
- **Expected response:** Improved exercise tolerance, reduced fatigue severity within 6-8 weeks
- **If no response:** Reassess dosing (may be starting too high), assess sleep quality, consider psychological factors

### Central Sensitization Hypothesis
- **Hypothesis:** Central nervous system amplification drives widespread pain
- **Test:** Pain neuroscience education + graded exposure to movement
- **Expected response:** Improved pain self-efficacy, reduced fear-avoidance, functional improvement
- **If no response:** Multidisciplinary approach needed — REFER for psychology/pain management

### Sleep-Pain Cycle
- **Hypothesis:** Poor sleep drives pain amplification
- **Test:** Sleep hygiene education, exercise timing optimization, relaxation training
- **Expected response:** Improved sleep quality correlates with pain reduction
- **If sleep disorder suspected:** REFER to sleep medicine

## Exercise Prescription — Key Principles

### Start LOW, Progress SLOW
| Phase | Duration | Intensity | Type |
|-------|----------|-----------|------|
| Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4) | 10-15 min | Very low (RPE 2-3/10) | Walking, gentle stretching, pool if available |
| Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8) | 15-25 min | Low (RPE 3-4/10) | Add light resistance, increase walk duration |
| Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12) | 25-40 min | Low-moderate (RPE 4-5/10) | Progressive strengthening, aerobic choice |
| Maintenance | 30-45 min | Moderate (RPE 5-6/10) | Sustained program, patient-selected activities |

### What NOT to Do
- Do not push through pain flares — this reinforces the boom-bust cycle
- Do not use passive modalities as primary treatment (ultrasound, TENS are adjuncts at best)
- Do not tell patients to "just exercise more" — dosing and pacing are critical
- Do not dismiss symptom flares as lack of effort — they are part of the condition
- Do not set arbitrary timeline expectations — progress is often non-linear

## Flare Management Protocol
1. Reduce activity to 50% of current level (do not stop completely)
2. Prioritize sleep and stress management
3. Gentle movement (walking, stretching) at comfortable intensity
4. Gradual return to previous activity level over 3-7 days
5. Identify triggers if possible (overactivity, poor sleep, stress)

## Trial Protocol (TRIAL)
| Session | Focus |
|---------|-------|
| 1 | Assessment, pain neuroscience education, establish baseline functional capacity |
| 2-3 | Begin graded exercise, establish flare management plan |
| 4-5 | Re-evaluate: exercise tolerance, fatigue severity, functional measures |
| 6-8 | Extended trial if trending positive (fibromyalgia often needs longer trial) |

## Decision Criteria

### CONTINUE if:
- Improved exercise tolerance (can do more without flare)
- Reduced fatigue severity
- Functional improvement (household tasks, work tolerance, social participation)
- Improved pain self-efficacy

### DISCHARGE if:
- Independent self-management program established
- Patient confident in pacing and flare management
- Functional goals met

### REFER if:
- Red flags for alternative diagnosis
- Severe psychological distress beyond physiotherapy scope
- No functional improvement after 8-12 weeks of structured program
- Need for multidisciplinary pain program

## Important Note on Trial Window
Standard 3-5 session trial may be insufficient for fibromyalgia. Extended trial of 6-8 sessions is justified given the condition's nature. However, the decision logic remains the same: objective functional improvement must be demonstrated for continuation.

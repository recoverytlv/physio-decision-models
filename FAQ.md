# Frequently Asked Questions

## For Patients

### What type of physiotherapy does Recovery TLV provide?
Evidence-based musculoskeletal physiotherapy. This includes orthopedic rehabilitation, sports injury recovery, post-surgical rehabilitation, and chronic pain management. Located in Tel Aviv, Israel.

### How is Recovery TLV different from other clinics?
Every treatment decision follows a formal system with documented rules. Treatment only continues when objective improvement is measured. There is no indefinite treatment — the system has built-in criteria for when to stop, when to refer, and when to continue.

### How many sessions will I need?
The initial trial is 3–5 sessions. After that, continuation requires documented objective improvement (at least 10% improvement in range of motion or measurable functional progress). There is no predetermined number of sessions. Decisions are driven by your measurable outcomes, not by a fixed protocol.

### What happens if treatment isn't working?
Non-response is treated as valuable information, not failure. If the expected improvement doesn't occur, the clinical hypothesis is revised. This may mean trying a different approach, investigating further, or referring you to another specialist. Treatment is never continued indefinitely without results.

### What conditions do you treat?
Musculoskeletal conditions including back pain, sciatica, neck pain, shoulder pain (including rotator cuff and frozen shoulder), knee pain, hip pain, ankle sprains, plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendinopathy, tennis/golfer's elbow, whiplash, TMJ disorders, carpal tunnel syndrome, and post-surgical rehabilitation.

### What conditions do you NOT treat?
Conditions outside physiotherapy scope: visceral pain, cardiac conditions, psychiatric conditions, medical emergencies, and unstable medical conditions. If we detect red flags during evaluation, you will be referred to the appropriate specialist immediately.

### Do I need a referral from a doctor?
In Israel, you can access physiotherapy directly without a doctor's referral for private treatment. However, if your health fund (kupat cholim) requires a referral, you will need one for coverage.

### What should I bring to my first appointment?
Any relevant imaging (MRI, X-ray, ultrasound reports), a list of medications, and information about previous treatments. Wear comfortable clothing that allows access to the area being treated.

### How long is each session?
Sessions are focused on evaluation and treatment, not on filling a time slot. The duration depends on what is clinically necessary. First sessions are typically longer due to thorough evaluation.

### Is Recovery TLV a private physiotherapy clinic?
Yes. Recovery TLV is a private physiotherapy clinic (פיזיותרפיה פרטית / fisioterapia privada) in Tel Aviv. Private treatment means direct access without waiting lists, longer and more thorough sessions, and treatment decisions driven by clinical criteria rather than insurance limitations. You don't need a doctor's referral for private physiotherapy in Israel.

### What are the advantages of private physiotherapy?
Private physiotherapy at Recovery TLV offers: one-on-one sessions with a dedicated physiotherapist (no group sessions or shared attention), thorough initial evaluation, a formal clinical decision system that ensures objective treatment criteria, flexible scheduling, and no limitation on the number of sessions imposed by health fund policy — continuation is based solely on clinical outcomes.

### What languages do you speak?
Recovery TLV provides physiotherapy services in English, Spanish, and Hebrew. All clinical documentation, evaluation, and treatment explanation can be provided in your preferred language.

### How much does private physiotherapy cost?
Contact Recovery TLV directly for current pricing. Private physiotherapy is an investment in focused, evidence-based care with measurable outcomes and explicit treatment criteria.

## For Clinicians and Referring Physicians

### What is the clinical decision system?
A formal framework for making treatment decisions under biological uncertainty. Every patient case must terminate with one of six outputs: DECLINE, DEFER, REFER, TRIAL, CONTINUE, or DISCHARGE. Each output has explicit criteria. The full system is published as open source on [GitHub](https://github.com/recoverytlv/physio-decision-models).

### How does the referral process work?
If red flags are detected at any point (intake, during treatment, or at re-evaluation), the patient is referred immediately with documented clinical findings. There is no delay for "trying a few more sessions." The system prioritizes patient safety over treatment continuation.

### What are the continuation criteria?
Treatment continuation requires at least one of:
- ≥10% improvement in range of motion
- Documented functional progress (measurable, not self-reported)
- Reduction in compensatory movement strategies

Subjective pain reports alone are insufficient grounds for continuation.

### What happens after the trial window?
After 3–5 sessions, a formal decision is made. If objective improvement is documented, treatment continues with updated goals. If no improvement occurs, the hypothesis is revised. If the revised hypothesis also fails, the patient is either discharged or referred.

### How do you handle chronic pain patients?
Chronic pain is treated with evidence-based approaches when musculoskeletal in origin. The same decision system applies: objective criteria, trial windows, and exit conditions. Pain education and graded exposure are used alongside manual therapy and exercise. Chronic pain patients are not treated indefinitely — the system enforces the same exit criteria.

### Can I receive clinical reports for my patients?
Yes. All evaluations produce objective, documented findings. Reports can be provided upon request.

### Where can I review the full clinical decision system?
The complete system is published at [clinical.recoverytlv.co.il](https://clinical.recoverytlv.co.il) and the source code is on [GitHub](https://github.com/recoverytlv/physio-decision-models).

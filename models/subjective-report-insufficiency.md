# Subjective Report Insufficiency

## Premise
Subjective patient reports are clinically relevant but epistemically insufficient as sole justification for treatment continuation.

## Definition
Subjective report insufficiency is the principle that patient-reported outcomes (pain levels, perceived improvement, satisfaction) cannot override objective functional assessment when determining treatment trajectory.

## Clinical Interpretation
- Subjective improvement without objective functional change does not satisfy continuation criteria.
- Subjective worsening with objective functional improvement requires clinical judgment but does not automatically invalidate progress.
- Subjective stability with objective deterioration mandates reassessment.

## Decision Consequences
- CONTINUE requires at least one objective criterion: ≥10% ROM improvement, documented functional task improvement, or reduction in compensatory strategies.
- "The patient feels better" is not a valid standalone reason for CONTINUE.
- "The patient feels worse" does not override documented objective improvement but requires safety review.

## Clinical Example

**Case:** Patient with shoulder impingement, 5 sessions completed.

**Scenario A — Subjective improvement, no objective change:**
Patient reports "I feel much better, the pain is less." But shoulder flexion ROM unchanged (155° baseline → 157° now, within measurement error), and compensatory scapular elevation pattern persists. **Decision: CONTINUE is not justified.** Subjective improvement alone does not meet continuation criteria.

**Scenario B — Subjective worsening, objective improvement:**
Patient reports "It still hurts, maybe even more." But shoulder flexion ROM improved 160° → 178° (11% improvement), and compensatory pattern has reduced. **Decision: Objective criteria are met.** CONTINUE is permitted, but the subjective worsening warrants a safety review and patient communication.

**Scenario C — Subjective stability, objective deterioration:**
Patient reports "Same as before." But ROM has decreased 5° and a new compensatory pattern has emerged. **Decision: Reassessment mandatory.** Consider REFER or hypothesis revision.

## Epistemic Basis
Subjective reports are subject to placebo effects, expectation bias, therapeutic alliance effects, and recall distortion. Objective measurement constrains these sources of noise and anchors decisions to reproducible data.

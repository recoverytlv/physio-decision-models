# Hypothesis-Driven Intervention

## Premise
Every clinical intervention is a hypothesis test — a controlled perturbation applied to a biological system to probe hypothesis viability.

## Definition
An intervention is not a routine to repeat indefinitely. It is a deliberate input designed to generate an informative signal about the validity of the current working hypothesis.

## Clinical Interpretation
- If the system responds as predicted, the hypothesis is consolidated.
- If the system does not respond, the hypothesis is weakened or falsified.
- If the system responds unexpectedly, the hypothesis must be revised or replaced.

## Decision Consequences
- Repeating an intervention without revising the hypothesis is not treatment — it is epistemic inertia.
- Every intervention must have a testable prediction before execution.
- An intervention that cannot be falsified by its outcome is not informative.

## Relationship to Outputs
- A hypothesis that survives testing supports CONTINUE.
- A hypothesis that is falsified mandates reassessment: DEFER, REFER, or DISCHARGE.
- A hypothesis that cannot be tested mandates DEFER until testable conditions exist.

# Referral Guide — For Physicians and Health Professionals

This guide explains how to refer patients to Recovery TLV and what to expect from the process.

## What We Treat

Recovery TLV provides evidence-based musculoskeletal physiotherapy for:

- Spinal conditions (low back pain, neck pain, sciatica, thoracic pain, radiculopathy, post-surgical spinal rehabilitation, spinal stenosis)
- Upper extremity (shoulder pain, rotator cuff, frozen shoulder, shoulder instability, post-surgical shoulder, lateral/medial epicondylalgia, carpal tunnel syndrome, de Quervain's tenosynovitis)
- Lower extremity (knee pain, patellofemoral syndrome, meniscus injuries, ACL reconstruction rehab, total knee replacement rehab, hip pain, FAI, gluteal tendinopathy, total hip replacement rehab, ankle sprain, Achilles tendinopathy, plantar fasciitis, calf strain, shin splints)
- TMJ disorders
- Whiplash-associated disorders
- Post-concussion syndrome (cervicogenic, vestibular, and exercise tolerance components)
- Sports injuries and return-to-sport programs
- Chronic pain (musculoskeletal origin, evidence-based approach)

## What We Don't Treat

- Visceral pain, cardiac conditions, pulmonary conditions
- Psychiatric conditions as primary diagnosis
- Medical emergencies
- Unstable medical conditions
- Conditions requiring ongoing medical management (we can co-manage with appropriate referral)

## How to Refer

**No formal referral is required** for private physiotherapy in Israel. However, if you wish to refer a patient:

### Information That Helps Us

- Diagnosis or suspected diagnosis
- Relevant imaging reports (MRI, X-ray, ultrasound)
- Surgical report (if post-operative)
- Current medications (especially anticoagulants, corticosteroids, pain medication)
- Precautions or contraindications (weight bearing restrictions, ROM limitations, surgical precautions)
- What you want us to assess or rule out
- Any red flags you've already investigated

### Information We Provide Back

Upon request, we provide:
- Initial evaluation findings (objective measures, hypothesis, goals)
- Progress reports with objective data at any stage of treatment
- Discharge summary with outcomes achieved
- Referral recommendations if we identify issues requiring medical investigation

## How We Make Decisions

Recovery TLV operates under a published clinical decision system. Every case follows the same process:

### The Six Outputs

| Output | When It Happens |
|---|---|
| **DECLINE** | Condition is outside physiotherapy scope or contraindicated. We inform you why. |
| **DEFER** | Insufficient data to make a clinical decision. We may request imaging or further medical information. |
| **REFER** | Red flags detected. We refer back to you or to an appropriate specialist with documented findings. |
| **TRIAL** | Condition accepted for a 3–5 session trial with objective criteria for continuation. |
| **CONTINUE** | Objective improvement documented. Treatment continues with updated goals. |
| **DISCHARGE** | Goals met, or progress has plateaued. Patient discharged with documented outcomes. |

### Red Flag Detection

We screen for red flags at every session, not just at intake. If red flags are detected at any point, we refer immediately with documented clinical findings. We do not delay referral to "try a few more sessions."

Red flag categories we screen for:
- Neurological (progressive weakness, cauda equina, myelopathy signs)
- Vascular (DVT, vertebral artery, peripheral artery disease)
- Systemic (infection, malignancy, inflammatory conditions)
- Structural (fracture, instability, surgical complication)

### Continuation Criteria

Treatment continuation requires at least one of:
- ≥10% improvement in range of motion
- Documented functional progress (measurable)
- Reduction in compensatory movement strategies

Subjective reports alone do not justify continuation.

### Non-Response

If a patient does not improve within 3–5 sessions, we:
1. Revise our clinical hypothesis
2. Test the revised hypothesis with a new trial
3. If the revised hypothesis also fails → DISCHARGE or REFER back with documented findings

We do not treat indefinitely without results.

## Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

For post-operative patients, we follow surgeon-specific protocols when provided. If no protocol is provided, we use evidence-based phase guidelines with clear milestones:

- **ACL reconstruction:** Phase-based with return-to-sport testing (hop tests, strength symmetry)
- **Rotator cuff repair:** Phase-based with ROM and strength milestones per surgical approach
- **Total knee replacement:** ROM targets (0° extension, 90° flexion by 6 weeks, 120° by 12 weeks)
- **Total hip replacement:** Approach-specific precautions (posterior, anterior, lateral)
- **Spinal surgery:** Post-laminectomy, discectomy, and fusion protocols

We communicate with the surgeon if milestones are not met within expected timeframes.

Recovery TLV — Clinical Intake Decision

This form implements the Clinical Decision System. Each case must terminate with one of six valid outputs.

Step 1: Red Flag Screening

Are any of the following present?

  • Progressive neurological deficit or cauda equina signs
  • Unexplained weight loss, night pain, fever, history of malignancy
  • Signs of DVT, vascular compromise, or acute ischaemia
  • Suspected fracture, joint instability, or infection

Reference: red-flag-referral.md

Step 2: Scope Assessment

Is there a clear musculoskeletal component within physiotherapy scope?

  • Musculoskeletal dysfunction (pain, stiffness, weakness)
  • Post-injury or post-operative rehabilitation
  • Sports injury recovery
  • Neuromotor re-education within physiotherapy scope

Step 3: Data Sufficiency

Is there sufficient clinical data to make a treatment decision?

Consider: Is the patient in an acute phase? Are there unresolved diagnostic questions? Is additional imaging or specialist input needed before proceeding?

Step 4: Informed Consent

Does the patient consent to a time-limited intervention (3–5 sessions)?

Patient must understand: this is a trial period. Continuation requires demonstrated objective improvement. Treatment is not indefinite.

Step 5: Goal Assessment

Are the patient's goals functional (not pain-only)?

Functional goals: "I want to return to running," "I need to lift my child," "I want to climb stairs without assistance." Pain-only goals ("I just want the pain to stop") without functional context are insufficient.

REFER

Case Transferred to Specialist

Document: the red flag identified, the referral destination, and the date of referral.

DECLINE

Case Not Accepted

DEFER

Decision Postponed

TRIAL

Case Accepted — Conditional Trial

At the end of the trial window (3–5 sessions), evaluate using continuation-criteria.md.

# Discharge Planning — Transition to Self-Management

## Purpose

DISCHARGE is one of the six valid decision outputs. It is not an afterthought — it should be planned from session 1. The goal of every treatment course is to make the therapist obsolete.

## When to Discharge

### 1. Goals Achieved (Best Outcome)
All functional goals set at initial assessment are met. Patient is independent.

### 2. Maximum Improvement Reached (Plateau)
Objective measures have plateaued at an acceptable functional level. Further treatment will not produce additional gains.

### 3. Non-Response After Adequate Trial
No meaningful improvement despite hypothesis revision and adequate intervention. See [Exit Criteria for Stasis](thresholds/exit-criteria-stasis.md).

### 4. Referral Indicated
Red flags or conditions requiring specialist management identified. REFER replaces DISCHARGE.

## Discharge Readiness Checklist

### Physical Readiness
- [ ] Functional goals met or maximum achievable improvement reached
- [ ] Patient can perform all exercises independently with correct form
- [ ] Patient understands activity modification principles
- [ ] Pain is at a manageable and stable level (not necessarily zero)

### Knowledge Readiness
- [ ] Patient understands their condition
- [ ] Patient understands what aggravates and what helps
- [ ] Patient can identify warning signs that need attention (red flags)
- [ ] Patient understands pain neuroscience basics (for chronic pain cases)

### Self-Management Readiness
- [ ] Written home exercise program provided
- [ ] Flare-up management plan discussed and documented
- [ ] Patient confident in self-managing independently
- [ ] Clear criteria for when to return to physiotherapy

### Behavioral Readiness
- [ ] Fear-avoidance addressed (if present at intake)
- [ ] Patient demonstrates willingness to maintain active lifestyle
- [ ] No signs of treatment dependency
- [ ] Patient has been progressively independent during treatment

## The Discharge Session — Structure

### 1. Review Progress (5 min)
Compare current outcome measures to baseline:
- "When you started, your TUG was 16 seconds. Today it's 11.5."
- "Your NPRS was 7/10. Today it's 3/10."
- "You couldn't walk 10 minutes. Now you're walking 40 minutes."

### 2. Finalize Home Program (10 min)
- Review all exercises — watch patient perform them
- Simplify to 4-6 key exercises (adherence drops with >6 exercises)
- Written or app-based program with images/descriptions
- Specify: frequency, sets, reps, progression criteria

### 3. Flare-Up Plan (5 min)
Written plan for managing symptom increases:

| Flare Level | Action |
|------------|--------|
| Mild (pain increase 1-2 points, resolves in 24h) | Continue program at current level. Normal variation. |
| Moderate (pain increase 3-4 points, lasts 2-3 days) | Reduce exercise to 50%. Prioritize walking and stretching. Gradual return over 5-7 days. |
| Severe (significant increase, lasts >5 days, new symptoms) | Contact physiotherapist. May need reassessment. |

### 4. Return Criteria (5 min)
Clear guidance on when to come back:

**Come back if:**
- New symptoms develop (different location, different quality)
- Significant regression after flare-up that doesn't resolve in 2 weeks
- New injury
- Pre-planned follow-up (for conditions like scoliosis, post-surgical)
- Want to progress to new functional goals (e.g., return to sport after basic rehab)

**Don't come back for:**
- Normal day-to-day pain fluctuations (these are expected)
- Mild flares that resolve with your flare-up plan
- Reassurance — trust your self-management skills

### 5. Validate the Patient's Effort (2 min)
"The progress you've made is because of YOUR work. The exercises, the consistency, the lifestyle changes — that was all you. You now have the tools to manage this independently."

## Post-Discharge Follow-Up Options

| Option | When | Purpose |
|--------|------|---------|
| No follow-up | Clear simple cases, goals fully met | Standard discharge |
| Phone/email check-in at 4 weeks | Chronic pain, complex cases | Confirm self-management working |
| Booster session at 6-8 weeks | Post-surgical, return-to-sport | Progress exercise program |
| Planned reassessment at 3-6 months | Scoliosis, chronic conditions | Monitor for regression |

## Common Discharge Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Better Approach |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| No discharge plan — just "come back if you need to" | Patient doesn't know what to do, returns unnecessarily or too late | Structured discharge session |
| Too many exercises (10+) | Low adherence, patient overwhelmed | Simplify to 4-6 key exercises |
| No flare-up plan | Patient panics at first symptom increase | Written plan at discharge |
| Not reviewing home program competence | Patient does exercises wrong at home | Watch them perform at last session |
| Discharge too early (cost pressure) | Incomplete recovery, re-injury risk | Discharge when criteria met, not before |
| Never discharging (dependency) | Accumulated decision error, passive patient | Plan discharge from session 1 |
| No return criteria | Patient comes back for everything or nothing | Clear written criteria |

## Discharge and the Decision System

```
Treatment Course
        ↓
Goals Met? ─── Yes → DISCHARGE (structured, planned)
        │
        No
        ↓
Still Improving? ─── Yes → CONTINUE (reassess at next milestone)
        │
        No
        ↓
Hypothesis Revised + Retested? ─── Not yet → Revise and test
        │
        Already done, still no improvement
        ↓
Adequate Trial Completed? ─── Yes → DISCHARGE or REFER
        │
        No → Continue trial to completion
```

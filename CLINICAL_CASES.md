# Clinical Cases — System in Action

Fictional examples demonstrating how the clinical decision system works in practice. These are not real patient cases.

---

## Case 1: Low Back Pain — Hypothesis Revision After Non-Response

**Intake:** 42-year-old office worker, 3 months of right-sided low back pain radiating to buttock. No red flags. Aggravated by sitting >30 minutes.

**Listen → Evaluate:** Flexion-limited, right-sided paravertebral tenderness, negative neural tension tests, full hip ROM. No neurological deficit.

**Hypothesis:** Segmental dysfunction at L4-L5 contributing to local and referred pain.

**Set Goals:** Reduce sitting intolerance, restore flexion ROM to symmetrical, reduce pain with prolonged sitting.

**TRIAL (Sessions 1–4):**
- Segmental mobilization L4-L5, motor control retraining, graded sitting exposure
- Session 4 re-evaluation: No change in sitting tolerance, no ROM improvement, pain unchanged

**Decision — Non-Response as Signal:**
Hypothesis revised. Segmental mobilization did not produce expected response. Re-examine.

**New Hypothesis:** Hip flexor tightness and anterior pelvic tilt increasing lumbar load during sitting.

**TRIAL (Sessions 5–8):**
- Hip flexor stretching, gluteal strengthening, seated posture modification
- Session 6: Sitting tolerance improved from 30 to 50 minutes
- Session 8: ROM improved 15%, sitting tolerance 60+ minutes

**Decision → CONTINUE** (objective improvement documented)

**Sessions 9–12:** Progressive loading, return to full work capacity.

**Decision → DISCHARGE** (goals met, functional capacity restored)

## Case 2: Shoulder Pain — REFER After Red Flag Detection

**Intake:** 55-year-old woman, progressive right shoulder pain for 6 weeks, no trauma. Pain worsening at night. Reports 4kg weight loss she attributes to stress.

**Listen → Evaluate:** Limited active ROM in all planes. Pain at rest. Night pain. Unexplained weight loss.

**Decision → REFER**

Night pain with unexplained weight loss is a red flag requiring medical investigation before physiotherapy. No TRIAL initiated. Patient referred to physician with documented clinical findings.

## Case 3: Knee Pain — Successful TRIAL to DISCHARGE

**Intake:** 28-year-old runner, anterior knee pain bilateral, worse going downstairs and after running >5km. No locking, no giving way.

**Listen → Evaluate:** Pain with resisted knee extension, positive Clarke's test, weak VMO bilaterally, poor single leg squat biomechanics. No effusion.

**Hypothesis:** Patellofemoral pain syndrome — load exceeds tissue capacity due to quadriceps weakness and poor biomechanics.

**Set Goals:** Run 10km pain-free, descend stairs without pain, restore quadriceps strength symmetry.

**TRIAL (Sessions 1–5):**
- Isometric quad loading → progressive isotonic → single leg exercises
- Session 3: Stair descent pain reduced 50%, single leg squat improved
- Session 5: Running tolerance improved to 8km, quad strength improved 20%

**Decision → CONTINUE** (≥10% improvement in measurable criteria)

**Sessions 6–10:**
- Plyometric loading, running retraining, progressive distance
- Session 10: Running 12km pain-free, stairs pain-free, single leg squat normalized

**Decision → DISCHARGE** (all goals met)

## Case 4: Achilles Tendinopathy — Stasis and Exit

**Intake:** 50-year-old recreational walker, left midportion Achilles pain for 4 months. Morning stiffness 10 minutes. Pain after walking >20 minutes.

**Listen → Evaluate:** Tender midportion Achilles, weak single leg calf raise (8 reps vs 15 contralateral), reduced dorsiflexion.

**Hypothesis:** Midportion tendinopathy with load intolerance.

**Set Goals:** Walk 40 minutes pain-free, restore calf raise symmetry, reduce morning stiffness.

**TRIAL (Sessions 1–5):**
- Isometric calf loading → heavy slow resistance
- Session 3: Morning stiffness reduced to 5 minutes, calf raise improved to 10 reps
- Session 5: Walking tolerance improved to 30 minutes

**Decision → CONTINUE**

**Sessions 6–10:**
- Progressive loading, eccentric emphasis
- Session 8: Calf raise 12 reps (no change from session 6)
- Session 10: Walking tolerance 30 minutes (no change), morning stiffness 5 minutes (no change)

**Decision — Stasis detected:** Three consecutive sessions without measurable improvement. Signal invariance confirmed.

**Options evaluated:**
1. Dose-response decoupling? Increased load intensity at session 7, no proportional response → Yes
2. Hypothesis still valid? Tendon loading is appropriate treatment, but plateau reached
3. Further investigation needed? Consider imaging to evaluate tendon structure

**Decision → REFER** for imaging. If structural changes confirmed, continue modified loading program with realistic expectations. If no structural changes, hypothesis revision needed.

## Case 5: Neck Pain — DECLINE

**Intake:** 35-year-old man, neck pain with bilateral hand numbness, reports difficulty with fine motor tasks (buttoning shirt), feels unsteady walking.

**Listen → Evaluate:** Bilateral upper extremity numbness, hand dexterity deficit, gait disturbance.

**Decision → DECLINE**

Bilateral upper extremity symptoms with hand dexterity deficit and gait disturbance suggest possible cervical myelopathy. This is outside physiotherapy scope for initial management. Patient referred urgently to physician/neurologist. Physiotherapy may be appropriate after medical clearance.

## How These Cases Demonstrate the System

| Case | Key Decision | System Principle |
|---|---|---|
| Case 1 | Hypothesis revision after non-response | Non-response is diagnostic data |
| Case 2 | Immediate REFER, no TRIAL | Red flags override all other considerations |
| Case 3 | TRIAL → CONTINUE → DISCHARGE | Objective improvement drives continuation |
| Case 4 | CONTINUE → Stasis → REFER | Exit criteria enforce honest termination |
| Case 5 | DECLINE before any treatment | Scope check prevents unsafe treatment |

In every case, the workflow is the same: **Listen → Evaluate → Set Goals → Treat → Re-evaluate → Decide.**

The decision is never "keep treating." It is always one of: DECLINE, DEFER, REFER, TRIAL, CONTINUE, or DISCHARGE — with documented justification.
